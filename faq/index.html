FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Does it matter if I vote?

Yes, it does! In NY, voters aged 18-29 make up 22% of all voters.

Posted by You(th) Can Vote on September 24, 2019

Where can I register?

You may register in person, online, or via mail.

Posted by You(th) Can Vote on September 18, 2019

How can I register In-Person?

Register to vote at a Board of Elections (BOE) office.

Posted by You(th) Can Vote on August 24, 2019

How can I register online?

Visit the DMV Electronic Voter Registration Application. Be sure to have a valid NY drivers license!

Posted by You(th) Can Vote on Aug 1, 2019

How can I register via mail?

Download the DMV Electronic Voter Registration Application. or call 1866.VOTE.NYC to have one sent to you for free. Once completed, send in to the Board of Elections at 32 Broadway, 7 Fl New York, NY 10004-160

Posted by You(th) Can Vote on July 23, 2019

What if I’m not allowed to leave work to vote?

The NYC voters board of rights states that you are allowed up to 2 hours to visit your polling site, during the day

Posted by You(th) Can Vote on July 18, 2019

What if I’m a student?

If you attend college/university in another state, you have 2 options. First, you may register to vote in your home state. This means that you will recieve an absentee ballot via mail. Your next option is to simply register in your home state.

Posted by You(th) Can Vote on July 18, 2019
